**On screen:** Iraq → civil proceedings → judgment affecting a non-party → third-party objection. Open the pleading checklist. Then briefly switch to criminal proceedings.

**Narration:**

“The new procedural assistant starts with the case track and exact decision. For a non-party affected by a civil judgment, it shows third-party objection, its eligibility questions, exclusions, filing authority, source, and drafting checklist. When I switch to criminal, ordinary appeal disappears; only the criminal routes encoded for that decision remain. No deadline or filing is guaranteed automatically.”

**Narration:**

“I select Iraq, the Civil Procedure Law, and article 305 on the court competent to issue an heirship certificate. The linked case uses fictional names, numbers, and dates. Its timeline separates three questions: article 305 for Iraqi court jurisdiction, Civil Code article 22 for the law governing inheritance, and Riyadh Agreement articles 25 and 30 for conditional recognition of a foreign judgment. The app never treats those rules as interchangeable.”

## Recording safeguards

- Keep the labels for published, synthetic, and catalog-only records visible.
- State that Salim, Layla, Amal, Noor, Saif, and Ruba are fictional and that no unverified appeal or correction decision was inserted.
- Do not call synthetic co-ownership decisions real Iraqi judgments.
- Do not claim that France or Egypt has a complete corpus.
- Show the app inside ChatGPT, not only the browser demo.
- Briefly show the actual Codex build session and the passing tests; do not claim a model or workflow that was not used.
- Show that criminal “appeal” is not offered as a generic Iraqi remedy and that the grievance route is specific to an order on a petition.
- Do not enter real client data into a pleading checklist.
- Use English narration or provide a complete English translation.
- Do not use unlicensed music, logos, client documents, or third-party footage.
- Keep the uploaded YouTube video publicly viewable by the judges without a permission request.
- Rehearse to finish near 2:48 and leave at least ten seconds below the official three-minute limit.
