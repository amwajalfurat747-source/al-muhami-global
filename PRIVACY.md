# Privacy Notice

## Prototype behavior

Al-Muhami Global's MCP tools are read-only. The server does not require user accounts, does not set cookies, and does not intentionally persist prompts, searches, selected procedural routes, draft-checklist choices, uploaded documents, or legal matter data. It returns information from the small multi-jurisdiction pilot corpus packaged with the application.

Standard infrastructure providers may temporarily process technical information such as IP address, request time, and error logs. Review the hosting provider's configuration and retention policy before a public launch.

## Do not submit confidential data

The prototype is not configured as a confidential legal case-management or court-filing system. Users must not enter client names, national identifiers, addresses, real case numbers, unpublished files, evidence, personal data, privileged communications, signatures, or other confidential material. Use fictional placeholders when working with the pleading checklists.

The templates provide sections and validation questions only. They do not file documents, calculate a guaranteed deadline, or create an attorney-client relationship.

## Sources and external links

Some results contain links to third-party legal sources in Iraq, France, and Egypt. Opening those links is governed by the third party's privacy policy and terms. A country appearing in the interface does not imply that its corpus is complete.

Legal text remains in its original language unless a separately sourced and verified translation is available. Records marked `DEMO` are fictional and must not be cited as judicial authority. In the cross-border heirship example, Salim, Layla, Amal, Noor, Saif, and Ruba are fictional names; all dates and identifiers are fictional or altered and do not reproduce a client's identity.

## Production requirements

Before processing real client or court documents, add authentication, access controls, encryption, retention and deletion rules, audit logs, incident response, a jurisdiction-specific data-protection review, and a clear controller/processor policy.
